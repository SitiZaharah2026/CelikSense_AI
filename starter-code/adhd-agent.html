Scientists have discovered that the human brain is incredibly adaptable. This remarkable quality, known as neuroplasticity, means that our brains can form new connections throughout our lives. Regular reading has been shown to strengthen these neural pathways. For learners with ADHD, short focused reading sessions are particularly effective. The key is to match the reading content to areas of genuine interest. When students read about topics they love, their brain naturally produces dopamine, which helps maintain focus. Try reading for just 5 to 10 minutes at a time, then take a short break before continuing. This micro-reading approach works with the ADHD brain rather than against it.
